Side Quests is a collection of small assignments that don't need to be part of something larger. Over the course of the school year, students can take these prompts to try and experiment with smaller design elements.

Unless otherwise specified, you can choose to make the subject up entirely, or you can use a previously existing organization. While some Side Quests might require a previously finished Side Quest, many are designed to be singular, one-off projects.

### Why Side Quests?

Personally, I never find I have enough time to experiment with my designs and would just jump into something safe that the client would like. While that's fine sometimes, it's important to explore and try different design styles – and I wanted to find something that I could use in my classes. However, I didn't want to deal with updating three different modules in our Learning Management System, and that's how this site came together; a central repository that would be easy to update using Github and Jekyll.

### Attribution

The idea of small projects is far from revolutionary. You do not need to ask permission to use these prompts. However, a link back to this site with attribution is apprectiated, but not required.
